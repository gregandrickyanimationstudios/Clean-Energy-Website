Solar Panels

Next Into our somewhat large adventure on clean energy are solar panels…

How does it work?

So what happens is that there are these things on the solar panel called solar cells, real creative I know, and in the cells are these things called semiconductors that are usually made out of silicon. When rays of sunlight hit those silicon atoms they get excited and start dancing around and, just like when you're dancing in a large group of people, they bump into each other and that creates electricity.

So each of those solar cells is producing power and when you put a lot of these six-inch by six-inch cells together you will get a solar panel often thirty-six to make a solar module. And then you put on a thin layer of plastic or glass over it. Then you take a couple of solar modules to get a solar panel, which can be serious.

Where does power go?

Well, that depends if you’re off or on the grid. If you’re off the grid, they’re perfect because you can store the power in a battery for the nighttime, but the battery can be expensive. Well, when you're on the grid it can work where the power the solar panels generate electricity gets sent to the grid for money called “net metering” or you can make the power the solar panel generates can lower your power bill.

What are the downsides?

The downsides? Well, it might be obvious, but they only work during the day unlike other things on this site.
